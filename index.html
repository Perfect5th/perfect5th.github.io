Web Design and Development – Minty Fresh

Hello! My name is Mitch Burton, and I make things on and for the web.

This site is a place where I can experiment, blog, and occasionally break things. My number one favourite thing to do is challenge myself to learn new things, so if you have an interesting project in mind, please let me know about it.

If something on here looks like hot garbage, or you just want to ask me something that won’t irreparably damage my self-esteem, you can contact me at mitch@mitchellburton.ca.

Feel free to sign me up for as many spam mailing lists as you like, I can take it. If you like the design of the site, let me know!

What I Do, Specifically Speaking

Oh, someone actually read this far? In that case, I’ll tell you in a bit more detail what I do.

Web Apps

My current full-time job is as a web app developer! I make stuff that does stuff. Neat stuff! Trust me!

I’m a full-stack web developer at Biolux Research, Ltd. in Vancouver, BC. This includes developing and maintaining a web portal, REST API, and online store as part of a small team. Being on a small team is great, as it lets me experiment with new technologies, and come up with creative solutions – turning my own and others’ ideas into reality.

Web Design

Design is a newer passion of mine, but I’m diving right into it!

As a member of a (very) small development team, having strong design skills is an absolute must. While I’ll admit that I am not the most experienced designer, I pride myself on a nearly fanatical attention to detail. I value clean, intuitive design, with an emphasis on consistency and reusability of components. Also, I really like green.

Data Analysis

Learning things requires data, and data is one of the things I do best!

With my background in biochemistry, I started my career in a research-oriented role. I have a high-level working knowledge of statistical analysis, which I do not hesitate to bore people with. Give me a big, fat dataset and throw me in a quiet room – I can’t promise you’ll get the results you want, but statistically-supported conclusions are a thing of beauty.

Technologies I <3

Here’s the rundown on the tech I know and love. This section should be constantly expanding. Otherwise, I’m probably dead – someone tell my family!

Python
Python is the language that I work in most frequently, as it’s the main one I use at my day job! I’ve listed it first here because it’s certainly my top competency. I use it for back-end development, (mainly Django) statistics, and automating boring tasks – sorry, optimizations.
JavaScript
JS is where it’s at right now, and I get the hype. I love JavaScript most when I want to make something quick, like a prototype or a bot that automatically responds “lol” on Slack. I slay with Node in the back-end and perform feats of derring-do with React (or Preact!) in the front. I also know jQuery, but I wasn’t going to mention that because I’m sure you don’t care!
R
Although most of my stats work nowadays has moved to Python, I still have a soft spot for R. When I need to do some quick hypothesis testing or gen a sassy multilevel regression, I’ll often dust off the ol’ R shell.
SQL
I know enough SQL to avoid stabbing myself in the eye with a tricky JOIN or subquery contstruct, but please don’t ask me to write your sprocs (at least, not yet). My preference is PostGres, and I’ll also use this space to mention that I’ve done some work with mongo, but I’m a novice at best. Isn’t this honesty refreshing?
HTML & CSS
I know both of these, yay! I’m a fan of Sass, and will use literally whatever template engine I can get my hands on.
Others?
I’m always working on learning something! Right now, those somethings are C, Rust, and maybe Go. If I feel I’ve learned something well enough, I’ll move it up to the list above.

I love writing documentation.
